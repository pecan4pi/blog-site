Work & falling into Lake Erie with a coworker

Quite the title isn't it? Lets first start off with my work, because your boy isn't unemployed anymore (kind of sadly, since now I can't say I'm that one unemployed friend).

So, I now have a job; a paid intership working on a lifeguarding boat as a deckhand. I really like boats & my school had a program where you could work for the company that funds (a lot of) & co-founded our school. There were options of where you could work, but I chose the lifeguarding boat since it was absolutely the coolest & most interesting option that I had. Since I'm really into boats & there are literally no other people in the class of 2029 at my school who are as devoted to maritime as I am, I was the only freshman who signed up for the lifeguarding boat. There were two other freshmen who did sign up for an internship, but they wanted to work on the cleanup boat. Either way, the boat patrols from a bit outside of the eastern [entrance/exit] of The Cleveland Harbor to The Rocky River area, as well as The Cuyahoga River. On the boat there are five people & unfortunately only 4 seats inside the cabin, although two people can sit out back on the stern, but thats outside. The five people are: the captain, the rescue swimmer, the paramedic & the two deckhands (interns from my school). Either way, first day the captain said it should be a pretty chill first day. Then, there proceded to be 3-4.5 foot waves in the harbor & 6-8 foot waves on open waters. 😭


Day one of working, 2026/5/30: 6 foot waves

First day it started calm & I was on shift with a classmate from 4th block math. If you remember me saying I was the only freshman & you were wondering why theres a classmate working with me, thats because most of the work in 8th grade I already knew from coding, so me & a few peers took Algebra I in 8th grade. Because of that, I took geometry in 9th grade; hence why I was working with a classmate. Continuing the story, me & my classmate, Bryson, were shown the basics of what deckhands do. Once we finally cast off (I looked it up, its not casted off), we found out that there were some pretty wild waves in the harbor (2-3.5 feet at that time). It wasn't that bad though, since our boat is very good. We were out going around the harbor for a bit before running into a massive tree. After some consideration, the captian decided that we tow it back to the marina & leave it for people who could deal with it to deal with it. After a good few attempts of tying up the tree (remember, there were strong, big waves moving us & the tree appart), we got it secured. We tied it to the boat & slowly drove back to the marina. By that time I was freezing my ass off since it was super windy, 58F & I didn't bring a jacket. The paramedic was nice enough to lend me his jacket & that helped a lot (although it was still pretty cold). Once we got there, we tied it to the wall & drove to The Cuyahoga River. On the way there I was still outside & we sped up. Someone said I should probably come inside & immediately a big splash of water came over the boat & hit me. From then on I ended up staying inside while we were going fast. Once we got to The Cuyahoga River, there was a bag so the paramedic (I think) asked for me to use the boat hook & grab it. I successfully got it, so put it aboard & continued on the river. We went around for a bit longer & then went out on the open water. Everyone was inside & we closed the door facing the stern aka the only actual door into the cabin. Then we started going, & boy did we go fast. Over the winter when the boat wasn't in the water, it got a big upgrade; 2 new motors for a top speed rivaling jet skis. I think this was the captains first time driving the upgraded boat, so he had some fun & so did everyone else. Once we got out of the harbor, the waves got wildly tall. I would say the waves got up to 8 feet tall, but the average one was probably 6 feet. Either way not much interesting happened besides us watching some surfers. However when we went back in The Cuyahoga, the highlight of our day happened; there was a massive (pool) flamingo in the water that flew out of a pool into the river. We saw it just after the rescue swimmer gave everyone some Starbursts & oh boy was it nice to be able to do our job. As soon as everyone saw it we all were kinda releived that something interesting happened & one of the crew members said "YES, WE FINALY CAN DO OUR JOB!" The captian turned on the siren to do a "woop" as he sped down the river (a no wake zone, but we are a lifeguarding boat so we don't apply during emergencies) everyone who could grabbed on to the flamingo & made sure it didn't fly away while we drove close enough to hand it over to some people to get it back where it came from. That was pretty much it, definitely not an uneventful first day, but I 100% had fun!


Day two of working, 2026/5/31: the boring day that day one should've been

Day two almost nothing happened. The waves were 1-2 feet on the open water, which shows how quick Lake Erie's weather can change. It was sunny & warm so I stayed outside for the entire day. I ended up getting sunburned, which my mom wasn't happy about. I also got a mad tan after the two 7 hour days on the water, which is cool I guess. We ended up driving down to The Rocky River & I learned that people that live there are stupid fucking rich, like they have fucking boat garages! Either way it was mostly boring, but I did get some practice tying some knots; the bolon, clove hitch & anothe one that idk the name for. That was pretty much it though.
