Work & falling into Lake Erie with a coworker

Quite the title isn't it? Lets first start off with my work, because your boy isn't unemployed anymore (kind of sadly, since now I can't say I'm that one unemployed friend).

So, I now have a job; a paid intership working on a lifeguarding boat as a deckhand. I really like boats & my school had a program where you could work for the company that funds (a lot of) & co-founded our school. There were options of where you could work, but I chose the lifeguarding boat since it was absolutely the coolest & most interesting option that I had. Since I'm really into boats & there are literally no other people in the class of 2029 at my school who are as devoted to maritime as I am, I was the only freshman who signed up for the lifeguarding boat. There were two other freshmen who did sign up for an internship, but they wanted to work on the cleanup boat. Either way, the boat patrols from a bit outside of the eastern [entrance/exit] of The Cleveland Harbor to The Rocky River area, as well as The Cuyahoga River. On the boat there are five people & unfortunately only 4 seats inside the cabin, although two people can sit out back on the stern, but thats outside. The five people are: the captain, the rescue swimmer, the paramedic & the two deckhands (interns from my school). Either way, first day the captain said it should be a pretty chill first day. Then, there proceded to be 3-4.5 foot waves in the harbor & 6-8 foot waves on open waters. 😭


Day one of working, 2026/5/30: 6 foot waves

First day it started calm & I was on shift with a classmate from 4th block math. If you remember me saying I was the only freshman & you were wondering why theres a classmate working with me, thats because most of the work in 8th grade I already knew from coding, so me & a few peers took Algebra I in 8th grade. Because of that, I took geometry in 9th grade; hence why I was working with a classmate. Continuing the story, me & my classmate, Bryson, were shown the basics of what deckhands do. Once we finally cast off (I looked it up, its not casted off), we found out that there were some pretty wild waves in the harbor (2-3.5 feet at that time). It wasn't that bad though, since our boat is very good.

We were out going around the harbor for a bit before running into a massive tree. After some consideration, the captian decided that we tow it back to the marina & leave it for people who could deal with it to deal with it. After a good few attempts of tying up the tree (remember, there were strong, big waves moving us & the tree appart), we got it secured. We tied it to the boat & slowly drove back to the marina. By that time I was freezing my ass off since it was super windy, 58F with little sunlight & I didn't bring a jacket. The paramedic was nice enough to lend me his jacket & that helped a lot (although it was still pretty cold). Once we got there, we tied it to the wall & drove to The Cuyahoga River. On the way there I was still outside & we sped up. Someone said I should probably come inside & immediately a big splash of water came over the boat & hit me. From then on I ended up staying inside while we were going fast.

Once we got to The Cuyahoga River, there was a bag so the paramedic asked for me to use the boat hook & grab it. I successfully got it, so put it aboard & continued on the river. We went around for a bit longer & then went out on the open water. Everyone was inside & we closed the door facing the stern aka the only actual door into the cabin. Then we started going, & boy did we go fast. Over the winter when the boat wasn't in the water, it got a big upgrade; 2 new motors for a top speed rivaling jet skis. I think this was the captains first time driving the upgraded boat, so he had some fun & so did everyone else. Once we got out of the harbor, the waves got wildly tall. I would say that some got up to 8 feet tall, but the average one was probably 6 feet, which is still wild. Either way not much interesting happened besides us watching some surfers.

However when we went back in The Cuyahoga, the highlight of our day happened; there was a massive (pool) flamingo in the water that flew out of a pool into the river. We saw it just after the rescue swimmer gave everyone some Starbursts & oh boy was it nice to be able to do our job. As soon as everyone saw it we all were kinda releived that something interesting happened & one of the crew members said "YES, WE FINALY CAN DO OUR JOB!" The captian turned on the siren to do a "woop" as he sped down the river (a no wake zone, but we are a lifeguarding boat so we don't apply during emergencies) everyone who could grabbed on to the flamingo & made sure it didn't fly away while we drove close enough to hand it over to some people to get it back where it came from. That was pretty much it, definitely not an uneventful first day, but I 100% had fun!


Day two of working, 2026/5/31: the boring day that day one should've been

Day two almost nothing happened. The waves were 1-2 feet on the open water, which shows how quick Lake Erie's weather can change. It was sunny & warm so I stayed outside for the entire day. I ended up getting sunburned, which my mom wasn't happy about. I also got a mad tan after the two 7 hour days on the water, which is cool I guess. The other deckhand was named Eamon, another 10th grader (going into 11th). We ended up driving down to The Rocky River & I learned that people that live there are stupid fucking rich, like they have fucking boat garages! Either way it was mostly a boring day, but I did get some practice tying some knots; the bolon, clove hitch & anothe one that idk the name for. That was pretty much it though.


Day three of working, 2026/6/13: the jet ski incident

Here we are, the main event of this blog; the jet ski incident. So, I should start off by saying that the boat was over full that day since we had [an observer/a shadow]. Because of that the rescue swimmer decided he would stop by the office & pick up the jet ski for him, which was a lifeguarding jet ski. I was working with an 11th grader, Phoenix, who is pretty chill, but sometimes a little unhinged. Over all though, he's really cool & knows a lot about most stuff to do with boating. Either way, the day was super uneventful until around 3:30pm.

Around then, we anchored about 40-100 feet away from the shore of Edgewater Beach (which was super packed & there were even some other boats anchored too). The rescue swimmer ended up meeting us there & asked Phoenix if he wanted to drive the jet ski, which he did. Phoenix drove around on the jet ski for a bit, doing jet ski stuff, but then came back. Also btw my heartrate is getting faster just thinking about this somehow lol. 😅 Anyways, he came back & then the captain asked the [observer/shadow] if she wanted to drive the jet ski. She said no, so then the captian asked me if I wanted to. I told him I wasn't 16 (you have to be 16 to drive a jet ski), so he asked if I at least wanted to ride on it with Phoenix, so I said sure.

I took off my shoes & got on. Someone asked if I really wanted to have my glasses on, but I shrugged it off, thinking I'd be fine. Phoenix said "your probably gonna want to hug me" before we started, but I just decided to hold onto his life jacket. Big mistake, That shit goes super fucking fast. We went for a bit, then when we slowed down to turn around I was like "hold on" & ended up hugging him. We went for a bit longer before hitting top speed. Before that though, he said I should probably stand a bit because we were about to go top speed, so I did & boy was he was right, standing does help a lot. I can only imagine how bad the sores would be if I hadn't stood a little.

After a bit more riding his hat flew off. This all happened very fast, but his right hand went off the handlebar towards his head, he stoped the jet ski & he turned his head to the right which caused the jet ski to turn. Then, it happened; we started leaning very quickly to the left & then boom, ~200-500 feet from shore, we fell in the water. For a second I was like, "oh shit, I'm in the water. I'm [fucked/dead]." since I can't swim very well. I started kicking my feet & moving my arms & I started floating. I thought to myself "oh shit, I might make it!" Then I resurfaced, opened my eyes, coughed up water, saw Phoenix looking [for/at] me & realized I had my life jacket on. I forgot that I had my life jacket on because I'd been wearing it for the entire day so I just stopped noticing it was there.

Either way, Phoenix told me to grab his hand & asked if I was ok. I was panicked because I thought I was [dead/about to die], but he ended up calming me down & also appologised a few times throughout the rest of the day. He then asked if I lost my glasses, which I realized I didn't have on. The main priority was getitng to the jet ski which was about 20-35 feet away I think. So we started swimming. Then I realized I had my phone in my pocket. There wasn't anything I could do about it now, so we kept on going forward. We eventually made it & I grabbed on to a type 4 pfd that was attatched to the back of the jet ski (remember, it was one for life guarding). Phoenix got on the the jet ski & then told me to get on. By that point I was exausted, so I used the last of my strength to get up behind him. Then I found out that there was a strap for the backseat rider. 💀 Safe to say, I held onto that on the way back, of which we went slowly.

Once we got back they asked us if we were ok. A bit later they told us they weren't worried & they saw the whole thing & found it hilarious. Phoenix was also a certified life guard so thats probably why, & thats also how he knew exactly what to do. Safe to say, I'm not trusting Phoenix with driving me on a jet ski again & when I told him that he said I never should've trusted him in the first place. Also later he said when I surfaced "It looked like he [thought he was dead/thought he was about to die], which was what was going through my mind. Finally, my phone had literally no damage whatsoever & worked perfectly fine somehow.


So those are the 3 days I've worked on the lifeguarding boat, aka my first job. Overall I think I'm going to have the most interesting summer stories to tell when school starts back up because of this. Btw if you were wondering what my pay is, its $13.50 an hour, with 7 hour shifts (9am-4pm) only on weekends. I think that wraps up this blog, so have a great rest of your day everyone & remember to not trust Phoenix with driving a jet ski lmao.
