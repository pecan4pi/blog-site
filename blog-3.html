Work & falling into Lake Erie with a coworker

Quite the title isn't it? Lets first start off with my work, because your boy isn't unemployed anymore (kind of sadly, since now I can't say I'm that one unemployed friend).

So, I now have a job; a paid intership working on a lifeguarding boat as a deckhand. I really like boats & my school had a program where you could work for the company that funds (a lot of) & co-founded our school. There were options of where you could work, but I chose the lifeguarding boat since it was absolutely the coolest & most interesting option that I had. Since I'm really into boats & there are literally no other people in the class of 2029 at my school who are as devoted to maritime as I am, I was the only freshman who signed up for the lifeguarding boat. There were two other freshmen who did sign up for an internship, but they wanted to work on the cleanup boat. Either way, the boat patrols from a bit outside of the eastern [entrance/exit] of The Cleveland Harbor to The Rocky River area, as well as The Cuyahoga River. On the boat there are five people & unfortunately only 4 seats inside the cabin, although two people can sit out back on the stern, but thats outside. The five people are: the captain, the rescue swimmer, the paramedic & the two deckhands (interns from my school). Either way, first day the captain said it should be a pretty chill first day. Then, there proceded to be 3-4.5 foot waves in the harbor & 6-8 foot waves on open waters. 😭


Day one of working: 6 foot waves

First day it started calm & I was on shift with a classmate from 4th block math. If you remember me saying I was the only freshman & you were wondering why theres a classmate working with me, thats because most of the work in 8th grade I already knew from coding, so me & a few peers took Algebra I in 8th grade. Because of that, I took geometry in 9th grade; hence why I was working with a classmate. Continuing the story, me & my classmate, Bryson, were shown the basics of what deckhands do. Once we finally cast off (I looked it up, its not casted off), we found out that there were some pretty wild waves in the harbor (2-3.5 feet at that time). It wasn't that bad though, since our boat is very good. We were out going around the harbor for a bit before running into a massive tree. After some consideration, the captian decided that we tow it back to the marina & leave it for people who could deal with it to deal with it. After a good few attempts of tying up the tree (remember, there were strong, big waves moving us & the tree appart), we got it secured. We tied it to the boat & slowly drove back to the marina. By that time I was freezing my ass off since it was super windy, 58F & I didn't bring a jacket. The paramedic was nice enough to lend me his jacket & that helped a lot. Once we got there, we tied it to the wall & drove to The Cuyahoga River. I think someone dropped a bag so I think it was the paramedic who asked for me to use the boat hook & grab it. We went around for a bit longer
